How to Make a Paper Rosette Lion Craft

What you need:

Yellow construction paper

Colored pencils

Googly eyes

Black markers

Glue

Scissors

Step by Step

Start by folding the piece of paper in half. After you fold it, go ahead and cut the piece of paper in half.
Once the piece of paper is cut, you can start fan folding the papers to make the Rosette. At the end, you should have two pieces of paper that are folded like a fan.
Take glue and put it on one side of the fan and glue the two pieces together. Then fold it in half and add a string to the middle and cut the string. Spread out the fan, the result will be a circle Rosette.
On a seperate pieace of paper draw a lion's face. Glue the face to the Rosette. Add the eyes and make a face on the lion.

How to Make a Paper Rosette Lion Craft

What you need:

Clean and Dry Recycled Milk or Juice Carton

White Paint

Recycled Cardboard

Foam Paintbrush

School Glue

Tissue Paper Squares in Assorted Colors

Twine

Wooden Spoon

Step by Step

Start by giving the recycled milk and juice cartons a light coat of white spray paint so that the package lettering wouldn’t bleed and show through the tissue paper. coat the sides of the carton with school glue and apply the tissue paper squares in any arrangement
After that is done applying the tissue, gently brush the tissue squares with the sponge brush to ensure that all the edges are glued down and secure. Allow to dry.

To create the roof, fold a piece of ther recycled cardboard in half width-wise. Cover the cardboard with glue and tissue paper squares. Set aside and let dry. If necessary, remove the plastic pouring spout with an X-Acto knife (adults only!). You can also cut off any excess cardboard from the “roof ridge” if desired.

Cut an opening for the door – if you would like to make a birdhouse, use a smaller sized opening; for a bird feeder, cut a large rectangular opening. About an inch below the door, use the X-Acto knife to cut two small slits in an “X” shape and insert the handle of a wooden spoon to create your perch. Before you push it all the way to the back of the carton, add a generous dab of glue to securely attach the end of the spoon to the interior back of the carton.

On your roof piece, cut two small slits about 2″ apart, and thread through the ends of your twine. Tie a knot securely on the underneath side so it won’t show. Using the school glue (or hot glue if you don’t want to wait for school glue to dry!), attach the roof to your carton.
